Brawl Shooters

Brawl Shooters is a top-down roguelike shooter developed in Unity using C#. The project focuses on implementing scalable gameplay systems such as enemy AI behaviour, wave spawning, player progression and weapon upgrades.

Project Overview

Brawl Shooters was developed as a gameplay systems project focused on designing and implementing the core mechanics required for a wave-based survival shooter.

The project explores how scalable gameplay systems such as enemy spawning, AI behaviour, and player progression can be structured to support continuous gameplay loops in roguelike survival games.

The gameplay loop revolves around defeating enemies, collecting experience points, and upgrading the player’s abilities in order to survive increasingly difficult waves.

Gameplay Overview

The player must survive waves of zombies inside an arena. Each wave increases in difficulty by increasing the number of enemies. Every five waves a boss enemy spawns with significantly higher health and damage.

  • Wave-based survival gameplay
  • Boss encounter every 5 waves
  • XP orbs dropped by enemies
  • Weapon upgrades when leveling up

Core Gameplay Systems

  • Wave spawning manager
  • Enemy AI chase behaviour
  • XP collection and leveling system
  • Weapon upgrade system
  • HUD elements for health, XP and waves

Wave System

The wave spawning system dynamically increases the number of enemies spawned as the player survives longer. This creates a gradual difficulty curve that encourages players to continuously improve their character through upgrades.

Boss enemies appear every five waves, acting as difficulty spikes that challenge the player's build and positioning.

Enemy AI Behaviour

Enemy AI uses a lightweight movement system where enemies constantly move toward the player’s position. Since the arena does not contain complex obstacles, a simplified chase behaviour was implemented instead of using Unity's NavMesh system.

This approach allowed for a large number of enemies to remain active without causing significant performance issues.

Player Progression System

Enemies drop experience orbs when defeated. Collecting these increases the player's experience level and allows the player to choose from different weapon upgrades.

This system introduces roguelike progression mechanics where players gradually become stronger during a run while enemy waves simultaneously increase in difficulty.

Performance Considerations

Wave-based survival games can quickly introduce performance issues when large numbers of AI agents are active simultaneously. To maintain stable performance, the AI behaviour was intentionally designed to remain simple and lightweight.

By avoiding complex pathfinding systems and using direct movement towards the player, the game can support larger enemy waves without placing unnecessary computational load on the engine.

Technical Challenges

One issue during development was enemies spawning too close to the player. Early versions occasionally spawned enemies directly on top of the player, causing unavoidable damage.

This was fixed by introducing a minimum spawn distance check when generating enemy spawn positions, ensuring that enemies spawn outside of a safe radius around the player.

Expected Outcomes

The project demonstrates how modular gameplay systems can be designed to support scalable difficulty and long-term gameplay loops in survival shooter games.

The systems implemented in Brawl Shooters also form the foundation for further experimentation with more advanced AI systems, such as the adaptive enemy behaviour explored in the Convergence project.
