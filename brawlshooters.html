Project Overview

Brawl Shooters is a top-down roguelike shooter developed in Unity using C#. The project focuses on building scalable gameplay systems such as enemy AI behaviour, wave spawning, player progression, and upgrade mechanics.

The design goal was to create a fast-paced survival arena where the player fights increasing waves of zombies while collecting experience orbs to level up and upgrade their weapon. Every five waves a boss enemy appears, introducing a difficulty spike and testing the player’s ability to manage positioning and upgrades.

Gameplay Systems

  • Wave spawning system with increasing enemy difficulty
  • Enemy AI behaviour that automatically tracks and chases the player
  • Experience and leveling system through collectible XP orbs
  • Weapon upgrade system triggered on player level-up
  • Boss encounters every five waves

Technical Implementation

The wave system dynamically increases difficulty by scaling enemy health and enemy count over time. Boss enemies appear every five waves and have significantly higher health to create pacing spikes in gameplay.

Enemy AI behaviour was implemented using a lightweight system where enemies continuously move toward the player without using Unity's NavMesh system. This decision was made because the arena does not contain obstacles, allowing the AI to remain performant even when large numbers of enemies are active.

XP orbs drop from defeated enemies and bosses. When the player collects them, their experience bar fills until they reach the next level, triggering an upgrade system that improves the player’s weapon performance.

Technical Challenges

One challenge during development was ensuring enemies did not spawn unfairly close to the player. Early versions occasionally spawned enemies directly on top of the player, resulting in unavoidable damage.

This was resolved by implementing a minimum spawn distance check when spawning enemies, ensuring they appear at least a fixed radius away from the player.

Another challenge was maintaining performance during large waves. To address this, enemy behaviour was intentionally kept lightweight and avoided complex pathfinding systems. This allowed the game to maintain smooth performance even on lower-end hardware.

Design Goals

  • Prioritise gameplay responsiveness over graphical complexity
  • Ensure the game runs on low-end hardware
  • Create scalable systems that can be extended with new enemies or weapons
