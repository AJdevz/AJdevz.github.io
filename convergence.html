Project Overview

Convergence is an experimental gameplay systems project developed in Unity that expands on the mechanics of Brawl Shooters. The goal of the project is to explore adaptive AI systems in wave-based games and investigate how enemy behaviour can dynamically evolve during gameplay.

Traditional wave-based games often increase difficulty by simply spawning more enemies. Convergence explores an alternative approach where enemies interact with each other and evolve into stronger forms through a merging mechanic. This creates emergent gameplay behaviour while potentially reducing the computational cost of managing large numbers of AI agents.

Research Question

How can adaptive AI systems, such as enemy merging mechanics, improve gameplay challenge and performance efficiency in wave-based games?

Research Inspiration

The project was partly inspired by the AI Director system used in Left 4 Dead, which dynamically adjusts enemy spawning and pacing based on player performance and game conditions.

Rather than only increasing the number of enemies, Convergence focuses on evolving enemy behaviour during gameplay through interactions between AI agents.

Core Systems

  • Adaptive AI behaviour system
  • Enemy proximity detection
  • Enemy merging mechanic
  • Dynamic enemy stat scaling
  • Wave spawning and difficulty progression

Adaptive Enemy System

Enemies are able to detect nearby enemies using proximity checks. When multiple enemies cluster together, they can merge into a stronger variant with increased health and damage.

This creates emergent behaviour where the structure of the enemy wave changes dynamically depending on how enemies move and group together during gameplay.

Performance Considerations

One of the motivations behind the merging system is performance optimisation. Large numbers of AI agents can become computationally expensive, especially in wave-based survival games.

By allowing enemies to merge into stronger variants, the system reduces the total number of active AI agents while maintaining gameplay difficulty.

Technical Challenges

One of the primary challenges was ensuring the merging mechanic remained readable and fair for the player. If enemies merged too frequently or unpredictably, it could make the gameplay feel chaotic.

To solve this, merging behaviour is triggered based on proximity conditions and controlled through cooldown logic to prevent excessive merging events.

Expected Outcomes

The project will evaluate whether adaptive enemy systems can create more engaging gameplay experiences while maintaining performance efficiency compared to traditional wave-based spawning systems.

The research findings will help inform how emergent behaviour systems can be applied in future gameplay programming projects.
